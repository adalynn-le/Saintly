Introduction

Word problems are the foundation for everything in mathematics-especially the AMC 10. By definition, word problems are "mathematical problem[s] expressed entirely in words typically used as an educational tool" (Meriam-Webster Dictionary). Due to their nature, word problems require you to exercise a different level of thinking and make connections between mathematics and language. The AMC 10, which especially focuses on applied and puzzling mathematics, incorporates these with other topics in order to challenge you.

select_check_box What is it?
A word problem is any problem that uses words to describe a mathematical situation rather than just equations. This involves simple and clear limits where the equation/constraints are described, or more commonly, situations explained via words that convey the numbers and operations nescessary.
select_check_box Why Study?
AMC 10 especially uses word problems everywhere because they encourage curiosity and test your thinking. Even outside the AMC though, word problems can model things in our lives and be used to represent real-world scenarios
